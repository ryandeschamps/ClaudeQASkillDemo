Test Scenario: New Hire FSA Election
Priority: High
Preconditions:
- FSA plan configured and active for new hires
- FSA calculator tool available in self-service portal
- IRS annual contribution limits configured in system
- Employee eligible for FSA based on benefits group
- FSA enrollment allowed during new hire enrollment period

GIVEN: A new employee (Thomas Miller) wants to elect a Healthcare FSA to save on taxes for anticipated medical expenses
WHEN: The employee uses the FSA calculator and enrolls in FSA during new hire enrollment
THEN: The employee successfully elects FSA with desired annual contribution amount and payroll deductions set up correctly

Test Steps:
1. Log into self-service portal as new hire (thomas.miller@company.com)
2. Navigate to Benefits Enrollment section
3. Select "Healthcare FSA" from available benefit plans
4. Click on "FSA Calculator" or "Estimate Your FSA" tool
5. Enter anticipated annual medical expenses (example: $2,400)
6. Review calculator results showing tax savings and recommended contribution
7. Review IRS annual maximum contribution limit displayed ($3,050 for 2024)
8. Enter desired annual FSA election amount ($2,400)
9. Verify system calculates per-pay-period deduction (example: $100 if bi-weekly, 24 pay periods)
10. Review FSA plan details, eligible expenses, and use-it-or-lose-it rules
11. Review carryover rules if applicable (up to $610 for 2024)
12. Confirm FSA election by clicking "Enroll" or "Add to Cart"
13. Review enrollment summary showing FSA annual election and per-pay-period deduction
14. Submit all benefit elections including FSA
15. Verify confirmation message with FSA election details
16. Check payroll deduction schedule reflects FSA withholding

Expected Result:
- FSA enrollment successfully completed with annual election of $2,400
- Per-pay-period deduction correctly calculated based on pay frequency
- FSA coverage effective date matches hire date (2024-03-10)
