Test Script ID: TS-013
Test Scenario: Spouse Loss of Coverage
Priority: High
Preconditions:
- Active employee currently not covering spouse
- Spouse loss of coverage life event configured
- 60-day enrollment window from loss of coverage date
- Loss of coverage documentation upload enabled
- Medical and dental plans allow adding spouse mid-year

GIVEN: An employee's spouse (Ashley Clark) loses their job and employer coverage on 2024-05-10
WHEN: The employee reports spouse loss of coverage and adds spouse to company benefit plans
THEN: Spouse is successfully added to coverage from loss of coverage date

Test Steps:
1. Log into self-service portal as employee (ashley.clark@company.com)
2. Navigate to "Report Life Event" section
3. Select "Spouse Loss of Coverage" as life event type
4. Enter date spouse lost coverage: 2024-05-10 (last day of spouse's employer coverage)
5. Verify 60-day enrollment window displayed (expires 2024-07-09)
6. Click "Continue" to begin life event enrollment
7. Verify spouse already exists as dependent (Michael Clark) or add if not present
8. If adding new dependent, enter spouse information and save
9. Verify action item created for loss of coverage letter/documentation
10. Navigate to benefit plan elections
11. Review current coverage: Silver HMO Employee Only
12. Change coverage level to "Employee + Spouse"
13. Review updated premium ($385.00 monthly for Employee + Spouse)
14. Optionally add spouse to dental coverage if desired
15. Review enrollment summary showing coverage additions
16. Submit life event enrollment elections
17. Verify coverage effective date set to loss of coverage date (2024-05-10)
18. Upload loss of coverage letter from spouse's former employer
19. Document should show coverage end date and reason for loss
20. Verify document uploaded with status "Pending Approval"

Expected Result:
- Spouse loss of coverage life event successfully reported
- Spouse (Michael Clark) added to medical coverage
- Coverage level changed from Employee Only to Employee + Spouse
- Monthly premium increased to $385.00
- Coverage effective date matches spouse's loss of coverage date (2024-05-10)
- No coverage gap for spouse between old employer and new coverage
- Action item for loss of coverage letter created and document uploaded
- Employee notified of coverage changes and new premium
- Payroll updated with new premium deduction
