Test Script ID: TS-065
Test Scenario: Create Arrears for Underpayment
Priority: High
Preconditions:
- Payment allocation completed
- Employee payment less than outstanding charges
- Arrears management functionality enabled
- Arrears tracking and collection configured
- Employee notification process for arrears

GIVEN: An employee payment ($200) is less than outstanding charges ($237) resulting in underpayment
WHEN: Payment allocation process identifies underpayment and creates arrears
THEN: Arrears successfully created, tracked, and added to next billing cycle for collection

Test Steps:
1. Employee payment received: $200.00
2. Outstanding charges for employee: $237.00
3. Underpayment amount: $37.00
4. Payment allocation process runs
5. System identifies underpayment for employee
6. System automatically creates arrears balance
7. Benefits administrator reviews arrears created
8. Navigate to "Arrears Management" or "Outstanding Balances"
9. Filter to show recent arrears: Created date 2024-11-15
10. Review arrears listing:
    - Employee: David Martinez
    - Person Number: P100030
    - Arrears amount: $37.00
    - Arrears date: 2024-11-15
    - Reason: Underpayment from batch payment
    - Status: Outstanding - To be collected
11. Click on arrears to view details:
    - Charges due: $237.00
    - Payment received: $200.00
    - Arrears created: $37.00
    - Collection plan: Add to next billing cycle
12. View employee's billing account:
    - November charges: $237.00
    - Payment received: $200.00
    - Arrears balance: $37.00
    - Status: Partial payment - arrears outstanding
13. System sends notification to employee:
    - Subject: "Outstanding Balance - Payment Short"
    - Body: "Your payment of $200 was less than charges of $237"
    - "Outstanding balance of $37 will be added to next month's charges"
    - "Please remit payment or arrears will be collected via payroll"
14. Next billing cycle arrives (December 2024)
15. New charges generated: $237.00 (current month premium)
16. System automatically adds arrears: $37.00 (from November)
17. Total December charges: $237.00 + $37.00 = $274.00
18. Employee's December payroll deduction: $274.00 (includes arrears)
19. Payment allocated:
    - December payment: $274.00
    - November arrears: -$37.00 (paid)
    - December charges: -$237.00 (paid)
    - Remaining balance: $0.00
20. Arrears fully collected:
    - Original arrears: $37.00
    - Collected in December: $37.00
    - Remaining arrears balance: $0.00
21. Generate arrears activity report showing:
    - Arrears created during period
    - Arrears collected during period
    - Outstanding arrears balances

Expected Result:
- Underpayment automatically detected during allocation
- Arrears balance automatically created for underpayment amount
- Arrears amount accurately calculated ($37.00)
- Arrears recorded with clear reason (underpayment)
- Arrears status shows "Outstanding - To be collected"
- Arrears visible in employee's billing account
- Employee notified of outstanding balance
- Notification explains arrears and collection plan
- Arrears automatically added to next billing cycle
- Total charges for next cycle include current premium plus arrears
- Employee's payment obligation increased by arrears
- Arrears collected through normal payroll deduction
- Arrears balance updated to $0.00 after collection
- Arrears activity tracked and reportable
- Audit trail captures arrears creation and collection

Test Data Reference:
- Underpayment scenarios
- Common causes:
  - Payroll deduction calculated incorrectly
  - Employee payment short of full amount
  - Mid-period enrollment change with retroactive charges
  - FSA election change mid-year

Notes:
- Verify arrears creation is automatic during allocation
- Confirm arrears amount calculation accurate
- Test arrears balance tracking and visibility
- Verify automatic arrears addition to next billing cycle
- Ensure employee notification clear and timely
- Test arrears collection through payroll deduction
- Confirm arrears collection via direct bill if applicable
- Verify arrears cannot be ignored or written off without approval
- Test accumulated arrears if not collected immediately
- Ensure interest calculation if arrears aged beyond threshold
