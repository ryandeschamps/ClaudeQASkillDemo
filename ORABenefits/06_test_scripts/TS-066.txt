Test Script ID: TS-066
Test Scenario: Place Billing on Hold
Priority: Medium
Preconditions:
- Benefits administrator role with billing hold authority
- Billing hold functionality enabled
- Hold reason codes configured
- Temporary hold duration configurable
- Automatic hold release capability

GIVEN: An employee experiences temporary financial hardship and requests billing hold
WHEN: Benefits administrator places billing on hold for one billing period
THEN: Billing successfully placed on hold, no charges generated, and automatically released after hold period

Test Steps:
1. Employee contacts benefits team: Temporary financial hardship due to unexpected medical expenses
2. Employee requests one-month billing hold to recover financially
3. Company policy allows one-time billing hold for hardship (management approval)
4. Benefits administrator logs into Oracle Fusion HCM
5. Navigate to "Billing Management" or "Billing Holds"
6. Search for employee: Person Number or Name
7. Employee: Jennifer Martinez, P100035
8. Review employee's current billing status:
    - November charges: $237.00 - Paid
    - December charges: Not yet generated
    - Billing status: Active
9. Click "Place Billing on Hold" or "Create Billing Hold"
10. System displays billing hold form
11. Enter hold details:
    - Hold reason: Temporary financial hardship
    - Hold start date: 2024-12-01
    - Hold end date: 2024-12-31 (one month)
    - Auto-release: Yes (automatically release after end date)
    - Management approval: Obtained (reference approval email)
12. Enter hold notes: "Employee approved for one-month billing hold due to medical expense hardship. Coverage continues, billing resumes January 2025."
13. Upload approval documentation (email from manager)
14. Review hold summary:
    - Billing hold period: December 1-31, 2024
    - Duration: 1 month
    - Charges affected: December 2024
    - Coverage status: REMAINS ACTIVE (coverage continues during hold)
15. Confirm billing hold
16. System creates billing hold: Hold ID BH-2024-12-001
17. Billing status updated: "On Hold - Temporary"
18. System sends notification to employee:
    - "Your billing has been placed on temporary hold"
    - "No premium charges for December 2024"
    - "Coverage remains active during hold period"
    - "Billing resumes January 2025"
19. December billing cycle runs
20. System skips employee during charge generation (on hold)
21. No charges generated for December
22. Employee's benefit coverage continues uninterrupted
23. January 1, 2025 arrives (hold end date)
24. System automatically releases billing hold
25. Billing status updated: "Active"
26. January billing cycle runs
27. Charges generated for January: $237.00 (normal billing resumes)
28. December arrears added: $237.00 (billing hold month)
29. Total January charges: $237.00 + $237.00 = $474.00
30. Employee notified of billing resumption with arrears payment plan option

Expected Result:
- Billing hold request process accessible to administrator
- Hold reason and duration clearly specified
- Management approval required and documented
- Billing hold created with unique ID
- Billing status updated to "On Hold"
- Employee notified of hold with coverage continuation assured
- Charges not generated during hold period (December)
- Coverage remains active during hold (no interruption)
- Billing hold automatically released on end date
- Billing status automatically updated to "Active" after hold
- Normal billing resumes after hold period
- Arrears created for hold period charges
- Payment plan option offered for arrears
- Audit trail captures hold creation, reason, approval, release

Test Data Reference:
- Employees experiencing temporary financial hardship
- Requires management approval for billing hold
- One-time exception-based accommodation

Notes:
- Verify billing hold is exception-based with approval required
- Confirm coverage continues during billing hold
- Test automatic hold release on end date
- Verify charges not generated during hold period
- Ensure arrears created for hold period after release
- Test payment plan option for accumulated arrears
- Confirm hold cannot be extended without additional approval
- Verify employee communication clear about coverage continuation
- Test hold removal before end date if circumstances change
- Ensure audit trail comprehensive for compliance and policy enforcement
