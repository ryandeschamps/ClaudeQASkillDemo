Test Script ID: TS-049
Test Scenario: Missing Certification Declaration
Priority: Medium
Preconditions:
- Employee unable to provide required certification document
- Declaration/attestation process configured as alternative
- Legal and compliance review of attestation approach completed
- Attestation form or process available in system
- Risk acceptance documented for attestation-based enrollments

GIVEN: An employee cannot provide required birth certificate for adopted child due to sealed adoption records
WHEN: The employee completes missing certification declaration (attestation) instead
THEN: Enrollment completed based on attestation, with appropriate documentation of alternative verification

Test Steps:
1. Employee enrolled adopted child in benefits
2. Action item created: Upload birth certificate or adoption papers
3. Special circumstance: International adoption with sealed records, documents unavailable
4. Employee contacts benefits team explaining situation
5. Benefits team determines attestation acceptable per policy for this scenario
6. Employee logs into self-service portal
7. Navigate to action items section
8. Select action item: "Upload birth certificate for adopted child"
9. Review action item details and document requirement
10. Click "Unable to Provide Document" or "Request Alternative"
11. System displays attestation option
12. Employee selects: "I cannot provide required document due to: [select reason]"
13. Reason options include:
    - Sealed adoption records
    - Document destroyed/lost
    - Foreign adoption without US documentation
    - Other (specify)
14. Select: "Sealed adoption records"
15. System displays attestation form
16. Attestation statement: "I attest under penalty of perjury that [child name] is my legally adopted dependent"
17. Employee completes attestation form with details:
    - Child's full name
    - Date of birth
    - Date of adoption
    - Adoption jurisdiction
    - Reason documentation unavailable
18. Employee signs electronic attestation
19. Upload any available supporting documentation (court summary, social worker letter, etc.)
20. Submit attestation
21. Attestation routed to benefits administrator for review
22. Administrator reviews attestation and supporting documentation
23. Administrator approves attestation as acceptable alternative
24. Action item status updated to "Completed - Attestation on File"
25. Enrollment proceeds based on attestation
26. Coverage activated for adopted child

Expected Result:
- System provides attestation option when employee cannot provide required document
- Clear reasons for attestation available and documented
- Attestation form legally sound with appropriate language
- Employee can complete attestation electronically with signature
- Supporting documentation can be uploaded with attestation
- Attestation routed for administrator review and approval
- Administrator can evaluate attestation against policy criteria
- Attestation approved as acceptable alternative to physical document
- Action item satisfied with attestation on file
- Enrollment completes successfully based on attestation
- Coverage activated for dependent
- Attestation clearly marked in system (different from document approval)
- Risk of attestation-based enrollment acknowledged and accepted
- Audit trail documents attestation, reason, supporting docs, approval

Test Data Reference:
- Special cases where documentation unavailable
- Scenarios: sealed adoptions, foreign adoptions, lost documents, destroyed records
- Requires policy allowing attestation as alternative verification

Notes:
- Verify attestation process legally reviewed and compliant
- Confirm attestation only available for specific scenarios (not general alternative)
- Test attestation language appropriate and legally binding
- Verify administrator discretion in accepting or rejecting attestations
- Ensure risk assessment done for attestation-based enrollments
- Test that attestation clearly distinguished from document approval
- Confirm periodic re-attestation required if document later obtainable
- Verify fraud prevention measures for attestation process
- Test that attestation option not used to circumvent necessary verifications
- Ensure compliance with plan provisions allowing attestation
