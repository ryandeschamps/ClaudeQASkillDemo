Test Script ID: TS-064
Test Scenario: Refund Excess Credit
Priority: Medium
Preconditions:
- Employee has credit balance exceeding future charges
- Refund request process configured
- Refund approval workflow enabled
- Refund payment method configured (check, ACH)
- Refund threshold defined (minimum amount for refund)

GIVEN: An employee has large credit balance ($500) that exceeds anticipated future charges
WHEN: Benefits administrator processes refund for excess credit
THEN: Refund successfully issued, credit balance reduced, and employee receives overpayment back

Test Steps:
1. Employee terminated employment but paid full month premium
2. Coverage ended mid-month after premium paid
3. Refund due: $250 for half month premium overpayment
4. Credit balance created: $250.00
5. Employee has no future charges (terminated)
6. Refund threshold: $50.00 (refunds issued for credits over $50)
7. Employee requests refund via benefits team
8. Benefits administrator logs into Oracle Fusion HCM
9. Navigate to "Credits and Refunds" or "Credit Management"
10. Search for employee: Person Number or Name
11. Employee: Michael Thompson, P100050
12. Review employee's credit balance:
    - Total credit: $250.00
    - Future charges: $0.00 (employee terminated)
    - Eligible for refund: $250.00
13. Click "Process Refund" or "Issue Refund"
14. System displays refund request form
15. Refund details:
    - Employee name: Michael Thompson
    - Credit balance: $250.00
    - Refund amount: $250.00 (full credit)
    - Reason: Overpayment due to mid-month termination
16. Select refund method:
    - Check mailed to address on file
    - ACH direct deposit to bank account
    - Other
17. Select: Check
18. Verify mailing address:
    - 123 Former Employee St.
    - City, State ZIP
19. Confirm address current or update if needed
20. Enter refund notes: "Premium overpayment - coverage ended 2024-10-15"
21. Submit refund request for approval
22. Refund requires manager approval (refunds over $100)
23. Manager receives approval request
24. Manager reviews and approves refund
25. Refund processed: Refund ID REF-2024-10-001
26. Check issued: Check #5678 for $250.00
27. Credit balance reduced to $0.00
28. Refund mailed to employee
29. Generate refund report for accounting
30. Update employee's billing record with refund details

Expected Result:
- Employee's credit balance clearly visible
- Refund eligibility determined (credit exceeds future charges)
- Refund request process accessible to administrator
- Refund amount calculated correctly
- Refund method selectable (check, ACH, etc.)
- Employee address verified before refund issuance
- Refund reason documented
- Approval workflow enforced for refunds over threshold
- Manager can review and approve refund requests
- Refund processed with unique ID
- Payment issued via selected method (check)
- Credit balance reduced by refund amount
- Refund tracked in employee's billing history
- Accounting report generated for refund
- Audit trail captures refund request, approval, issuance

Test Data Reference:
- Terminated employees with credit balances
- Scenarios requiring refunds:
  - Mid-period terminations with full payment
  - Large overpayments
  - Plan changes with retroactive rate reductions
  - Divorced spouse removed with prepaid premium

Notes:
- Verify refund threshold configurable (e.g., $50 minimum)
- Confirm approval workflow for large refunds
- Test various refund methods (check, ACH, wire)
- Verify address verification before check issuance
- Ensure refund reduces credit balance correctly
- Test partial refunds (keeping some credit for anticipated charges)
- Confirm refund reporting for accounting reconciliation
- Verify tax implications documented if applicable
- Test escheatment rules for unclaimed refunds
- Ensure audit trail comprehensive for compliance
