Test Script ID: TS-046
Test Scenario: Interim Coverage During Suspension
Priority: High
Preconditions:
- Interim coverage rules configured for document review periods
- Employee enrolled dependent with pending document
- Grace period or interim coverage period defined
- Coverage levels for interim period specified
- Transition from interim to full coverage automated

GIVEN: An employee enrolled a dependent and uploaded documentation pending administrator approval
WHEN: The document is under review during the interim period
THEN: Employee and dependent have interim coverage while verification is pending, then transition to full coverage upon approval

Test Steps:
1. Employee enrolls spouse in medical benefits during marriage life event
2. Uploads marriage certificate for verification
3. Document status: "Pending Approval"
4. System grants interim coverage during document review period (up to 90 days)
5. Interim coverage rules applied:
   - Basic coverage active (emergency services, primary care)
   - Some services may require pre-authorization
   - Full coverage pending document approval
6. Employee logs into self-service portal
7. View benefits summary showing coverage status
8. Coverage displays as: "Active - Interim Coverage (Pending Document Verification)"
9. Click for details about interim coverage
10. System explains:
    - Spouse has interim coverage for basic services
    - Full coverage will activate upon document approval
    - Estimated approval timeframe: 5-10 business days
11. Employee and spouse can use interim coverage:
    - Primary care visits covered
    - Emergency services covered
    - Preventive care covered
    - Specialist visits may require pre-authorization
    - Elective procedures may be held pending full coverage
12. Review insurance ID cards: Marked as "Interim Coverage"
13. After 7 days, administrator approves marriage certificate
14. System automatically transitions from interim to full coverage
15. Coverage status updates to: "Active - Full Coverage"
16. Employee receives notification: "Your spouse coverage is now fully active"
17. New insurance ID cards issued without interim restriction
18. All services now covered per plan benefits
19. Any held claims processed with full coverage
20. Premium remains consistent (no change between interim and full coverage)

Expected Result:
- Interim coverage automatically activated upon document submission
- Coverage status clearly indicates "Interim Coverage" during review period
- Interim coverage limitations clearly explained to employee
- Basic and emergency services covered during interim period
- Insurance ID cards issued indicating interim status
- Employee and dependent can access care during document review
- Provider network access available during interim coverage
- Automatic transition from interim to full coverage upon document approval
- No gap in coverage between enrollment and full coverage activation
- Employee notified when full coverage activated
- New ID cards issued for full coverage
- Claims processing seamless during transition
- Premium deductions consistent throughout (no interim discount)
