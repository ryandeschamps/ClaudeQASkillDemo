Test Script ID: TS-027
Test Scenario: Open Enrollment - FSA Restart
Priority: Critical
Preconditions:
- Active open enrollment period
- Employee had FSA in prior year
- FSA requires annual re-election (use-it-or-lose-it rule)
- FSA calculator available
- IRS annual contribution limits configured for new year

GIVEN: An employee (Rebecca Baker) with FSA in current year wants to continue FSA for next year
WHEN: The employee re-elects FSA contribution amount during open enrollment
THEN: FSA successfully re-elected for new plan year with new annual contribution

Test Steps:
1. Employee logs into self-service portal during open enrollment (rebecca.baker@company.com)
2. Navigate to Open Enrollment section
3. Click "Start Open Enrollment" or "Make Changes"
4. Review current year FSA: Healthcare FSA with $2,400 annual election
5. System displays notice: "FSA requires annual re-election - it does NOT automatically continue"
6. Navigate to FSA section
7. Review FSA options: Healthcare FSA and Dependent Care FSA
8. Select Healthcare FSA
9. Review current year election amount: $2,400
10. Access FSA calculator to estimate needs for next year
11. Enter anticipated medical expenses for 2025: doctor visits, prescriptions, dental, vision
12. Calculator shows recommended election: $2,850
13. Review IRS annual maximum for 2025: $3,200
14. Enter new FSA election amount: $2,850
15. System calculates per-pay-period deduction (example: $118.75 if bi-weekly)
16. Review FSA rules: use-it-or-lose-it with $640 carryover allowed (2025 limit)
17. Review eligible expenses list
18. Confirm FSA election
19. Review enrollment summary showing FSA re-election
20. Submit open enrollment elections
21. Verify FSA effective date: 2025-01-01 with full annual amount available

Expected Result:
- System clearly communicates FSA requires annual re-election
- Current year FSA information displayed for reference
- FSA calculator accessible and provides helpful guidance
- IRS annual maximum clearly displayed ($3,200 for 2025)
- Employee successfully elects FSA for new year: $2,850 annually
- Per-pay-period deduction correctly calculated
- Carryover rules explained (up to $640 from 2024 to 2025)
- Use-it-or-lose-it rule clearly communicated
- FSA effective date: 2025-01-01
- Full annual amount ($2,850) available on first day of plan year
- Confirmation includes FSA details and eligible expenses
- Payroll updated with new FSA deduction amount
