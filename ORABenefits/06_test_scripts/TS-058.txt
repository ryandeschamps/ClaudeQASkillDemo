Test Script ID: TS-058
Test Scenario: Dependent Eligibility Verification
Priority: Medium
Preconditions:
- Benefits administrator role with verification authority
- Dependent eligibility audit program configured
- Verification request process enabled
- Employees can submit eligibility documentation
- Ineligible dependent removal process defined

GIVEN: Benefits administrator conducts dependent eligibility audit
WHEN: Administrator requests verification of dependent eligibility and reviews submitted documentation
THEN: Eligible dependents verified and remain on coverage; ineligible dependents identified for removal

Test Steps:
1. Benefits administrator plans annual dependent eligibility audit
2. Administrator logs into Oracle Fusion HCM
3. Navigate to "Dependent Eligibility Audit" or "Verification Campaign"
4. Click "Create New Verification Campaign"
5. Configure audit campaign:
   - Name: "2024 Annual Dependent Eligibility Verification"
   - Target population: All employees with dependents
   - Verification period: 60 days
   - Required documents: Birth certificates, marriage certificates, tax returns, etc.
6. Select verification method:
   - Self-service portal upload
   - Third-party verification vendor
   - Mail-in documentation
7. Launch verification campaign
8. System generates verification requests to all employees with dependents
9. Employees receive notification:
   - Subject: "Action Required - Verify Dependent Eligibility"
   - Instructions for submitting verification documents
   - Deadline: 60 days
   - Consequences: Ineligible dependents will be removed
10. Employee logs in and uploads required documents:
    - Marriage certificate for spouse
    - Birth certificates for children
    - Recent tax return showing dependents
11. Benefits administrator reviews submissions
12. Navigate to "Verification Review Queue"
13. Review first employee's submission:
    - Employee: John Smith
    - Dependents: Spouse and 2 children
    - Documents submitted: Marriage cert, 2 birth certs, tax return
14. Verify documents authentic and dependents meet eligibility criteria
15. Approve verification: All dependents verified as eligible
16. Move to next employee submission
17. Review problematic submission:
    - Employee: Jane Doe
    - Dependent: Adult child age 28
    - Documents: Birth certificate shows child age 28 (over age limit of 26)
    - No full-time student certification
    - No disabled dependent certification
18. Dependent identified as ineligible (exceeds age limit)
19. Mark dependent for removal
20. System generates notice to employee:
    - "Your dependent [child name] does not meet eligibility criteria"
    - "Coverage will end on [date]"
    - "Please contact benefits if you believe this is in error"
21. Process removal of ineligible dependent
22. Update coverage from Employee+Children to Employee Only
23. Adjust premium accordingly
24. At campaign end, generate audit results report:
    - Total employees audited
    - Verification completion rate
    - Ineligible dependents identified and removed
    - Cost savings from removing ineligible dependents

Expected Result:
- Eligibility verification campaign successfully created and launched
- All employees with dependents receive verification requests
- Clear instructions and deadline communicated
- Employees can submit verification documents via self-service portal
- Administrator can efficiently review submissions in queue
- Document authenticity and dependent eligibility verified
- Eligible dependents confirmed and coverage continues
- Ineligible dependents identified through document review
- Removal process initiated for ineligible dependents
- Employees notified of ineligible dependent determinations
- Appeal process available if employee disagrees
- Coverage and premiums adjusted for removed dependents
- Audit results reported with metrics and cost savings
- Compliance with plan provisions and regulations maintained

Test Data Reference:
- All employees with Number_Of_Dependents > 0
- Various scenarios:
  - Eligible dependents with proper documentation
  - Children approaching age limits
  - Adult children requiring student/disabled status
  - Divorced spouses incorrectly remaining on coverage

Notes:
- Verify audit campaign process comprehensive and efficient
- Confirm clear communication to employees about requirements and deadlines
- Test grace period for document submission (extensions if needed)
- Verify appeal process for disputed determinations
- Ensure removal process includes proper notice period
- Test that verified eligibility documented and stored
- Confirm periodic re-verification required (annual or biennial)
- Verify cost savings tracking for audit program ROI
- Test that eligible dependents not disrupted by audit
- Ensure compliance with plan provisions and legal requirements
